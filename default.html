Speech Frame

very small chunks/clips of audio, to make processing easier

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

A Speech Frame is a small chunk/clip of audio from speech that is used by speaker encoders to create speaker embeddings.

All of these are concepts in speaker recognition.

Chunks

A speech frame isn't measure in bytes, instead they are measure in (units of) time. Usually milliseconds.

The common length for a speech frame is: 20ms.

20ms long speech frames are very common.

A reason for this length is — if you zoom in to human speech waveforms, you find that at the scale of about 20ms to 30ms the wafeform look relatively constant. That means if were divide up speech into, for example, 20ms chunks, then within each of those chunks, most of them will look constant (within the chunk).
